GDPR Compliance

This page describes how QR Generator Pro approaches compliance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and similar data‑protection laws.

1. Data Controller

The entity responsible for your personal data (the "data controller") is the operating company behind QR Generator Pro. Contact details are available in our Privacy Policy and on our contact page.

2. Lawful Bases for Processing

We process personal data only when we have a lawful basis to do so, including:

  • Contract: to provide and manage your account, subscriptions and usage of the Service.
  • Legitimate interests: to secure our platform, prevent abuse and improve the Service.
  • Legal obligation: to comply with tax, accounting and financial‑services regulations.
  • Consent: for optional communications such as newsletters or beta programmes.

3. Data Subject Rights

If you are located in the EU/EEA, UK or another region that grants similar rights, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Request deletion of your data, subject to legal retention obligations.
  • Object to or restrict certain processing activities.
  • Receive a copy of your data in a structured, commonly used and machine‑readable format.

You can make a request by emailing privacy@text2qr.online. We respond to all valid requests as required by law.

4. Sub‑processors and International Transfers

We use carefully selected sub‑processors (for example, cloud infrastructure providers and payment processors) to deliver the Service. When personal data is transferred outside the EU/EEA or UK, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent mechanisms.

5. Data Security

We implement technical and organisational measures designed to protect personal data, including encryption in transit, access controls and regular security updates. We also monitor for abuse and suspicious activity and may suspend accounts that pose a security risk.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal data for as long as necessary to provide the Service and meet legal and regulatory obligations. When data is no longer required, it is anonymised or securely deleted.

7. Records and Audits

We maintain internal records of processing activities and data‑processing agreements with our sub‑processors, as required by GDPR. Payment gateways may rely on this documentation when assessing the compliance of our platform.

8. Contact and Complaints

If you have questions about our GDPR compliance, please contact us at privacy@text2qr.online. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data‑protection authority.

This page summarises our GDPR approach for users and payment gateways. It does not replace the full information in our Privacy Policy and does not constitute legal advice.
